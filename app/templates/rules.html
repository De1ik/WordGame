Wordle Rules

Wordle follows elegantly simple rules.

Your main objective is to guess a secret word that is either 5, 6, or 7 letters long using as few guesses as possible.

To make a guess, you type a word with n-number of letters and press enter. However, all guesses must be real words that our game's dictionary allows; you can't simply make up a non-existent word like "CFHSVF" in an attempt to guess certain letters.

Once you've submitted your guess, the game will provide feedback by color-coding each letter, indicating how close your guess was to the letters in the hidden word:

  • A blue square means the letter does not appear in the secret word at all.
  • A yellow square means the letter is present in the secret word, but it's in the wrong position within the word.
  • A green square means the letter appears in the secret word, and it's in the correct position.

By receiving green or yellow squares, you get closer to guessing the actual secret word, as it indicates you've correctly identified some letters.

For instance, if your guess is FELLOW and you receive two green squares on the F, with yellow squares for the L and with gray squares for the E, O, and W, your next guess might be FACTOR, FAILLE, or FAMILY, as these words start with F, includes L and don't contain the letters E, O, or W.

Remember, the same letter can appear multiple times in the secret word, but there's no special color coding for letters that appear repeatedly. For example, if the secret word is PEOPLE and you guess a word with one P and one E, game won't inform you that both those letters appear twice in the secret word, but you'll be able to track it with the auxiliary blocks.
